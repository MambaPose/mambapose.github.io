Abstract

This project focuses on enhancing the accuracy and efficiency of optical flow and depth estimation techniques in computer vision, which are pivotal for comprehending motion and scene structure. These capabilities are essential across a variety of applications, from autonomous driving to augmented reality. Despite significant advancements, existing methods often face challenges in complex scenes, dealing with occlusions, and operating within textureless regions. This has led to a concerted effort to develop more sophisticated methodologies.

Our proposed solution integrates state-of-the-art techniques, specifically RAFT for optical flow estimation, Depth Anything for depth estimation, and MambaPose for advanced pose estimation. RAFT is renowned for its ability to capture detailed motion patterns with high accuracy. Depth Anything provides versatile depth estimation from a range of input modalities. MambaPose enhances this suite by offering precise 3D pose estimation capabilities, crucial for understanding the dynamics and geometry of scenes involving human subjects.

Methodologically, we leverage RAFT, Depth Anything, and MambaPose, optimizing their performance for real-world applications. This involves meticulous experimentation and parameter tuning to ensure the robustness and efficiency of our solutions. MambaPose, in particular, is integrated to enhance the system's ability to interpret complex human movements within dynamic environments, making it highly relevant for scenarios where human interaction is significant.

The results of this integrated approach demonstrate significant improvements in optical flow accuracy, depth estimation quality, and pose estimation precision compared to baseline methods. Our methods exhibit superior performance in challenging conditions, including fast motion, occlusions, and textureless areas. The inclusion of MambaPose adds an additional layer of functionality by accurately capturing human poses, which is critical for applications such as interactive gaming, sports analytics, and real-time surveillance.

Overall, this work contributes to advancing the state-of-the-art in computer vision by enhancing the accuracy and efficiency of optical flow, depth estimation, and human pose estimation techniques. Through these integrated solutions, we aim to provide a more comprehensive understanding of scene dynamics and geometry, pushing the boundaries of what is achievable in complex and interaction-rich environments.

Depth

We sought to capture the very essence of the 3D scene – the distance of each pixel from the camera in every frame. This crucial information, aptly named depth, unlocks a deeper understanding of the environment. To achieve this, we employed a pre-trained depth estimation model known as Depth Anything.

Optical Flow

We aim to determine the apparent motion of pixels between consecutive frames within a video sequence. This motion information is crucial for understanding object and body movements in the scene.
